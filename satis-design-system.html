Numerals — always Literata, even inside system text

Letters are system sans; every digit is Literata. The numbers gently lift off the sans body and pull the eye — appropriate when the data is the point. This whole page uses the production technique — a digit-only unicode-range @font-face layered ahead of the system stack — so every number in content (captions, chart axes, badges, nav, card data) is Literata automatically, no per-number markup. Exception: monospace technical literals (hex codes, the type-spec column) stay monospace — they're code, not data.

Charts

Two colouring modes, kept distinct: state colouring (one metric vs its target — uses the progress-ramp colours: sage / honey / success, red over a cap) and series colouring (telling multiple metrics apart — uses the subtle series palette below). More colour than before, but every hue earns its place and stays legible on the dark surface.

Success & completion

The reward colour — Spring #2fce85 (token --success; text/icons on it use --on-success #06281a). Reserved strictly for the moment of a win — checks, completions, targets hit. Brighter than sage on purpose so the tick feels satisfying, and disciplined: it appears only at success, never decoratively. The governing rule: sage = "in progress / on track"; success green = "done / hit."
