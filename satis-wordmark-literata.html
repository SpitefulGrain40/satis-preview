The full stop is doing real work here — "Satis." reads as "enough. full stop," which is the entire brand thesis in one mark. It also gives the wordmark a definite end, balancing the underline at the start. My pick: Literata 500, 8px tracking, with the stop — refined without being precious. The period's spacing is pulled in slightly (negative margin) so it reads as punctuation, not a sixth letter. Watch on a real screen whether the centred underline feels right under the S bowl, or wants to nudge a hair narrower.

My read: Literata gives a single-serif identity — the wordmark, headings, voice, and nav all speak in one face. That's more cohesive and more "understated literary," and it ties the logo directly to the reading experience. The trade-off: Literata is a text face, so as a wordmark it's quieter and less couture than the Playfair+Cormorant lockup — that combo has dramatic thick/thin contrast and a fashion-house confidence that Literata deliberately doesn't.

Net: Playfair+Cormorant = more distinctive logo, two extra fonts to load, logo-only. Literata = quieter but more unified, one fewer font family overall, and the wordmark literally shares DNA with every heading. For a brand whose whole ethos is "enough / calm / unfussy," the Literata route is more honest to the philosophy — but the current lockup has more standout shelf-presence. Worth deciding which matters more here.
